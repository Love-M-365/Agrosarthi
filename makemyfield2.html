Pearl Millet Cultivation Guide

Sowing:
Kharif: First fortnight of July (north and central India).
Rabi: First fortnight of October (Tamil Nadu).
Marathwada: Dry sowing before monsoon rains.
Summer: Last week of January to 1st week of February (zone B).
Seed Treatment:
Biopesticides: Trichoderma harzianum (4g/kg seed) for soilborne diseases. Thiram 75%
dust: 3g/kg seed for soilborne diseases.
Sulfur powder: 300-mesh sulfur powder (4g/kg seed) for smut disease.
Salt solution: 10% salt solution to remove ergot-affected seeds.
Metalaxyl (Apron 35 SD): 6g/kg seed for downy mildew.
Azospirillum and Phosphobacterium: 600g/ha to enhance nutrient availability. Spacing and Plant Population:
Arid-western plain (A1 zone): 60 cm row spacing, 1-1.25 lakh plants/ha.
Rainfall > 450 mm (zones A & B): 45 x 10-15 cm spacing, 1.75-2 lakh plants/ha.
Seed rate: 3-4 kg/ha.
Recommended plant population:
180,000 plants/ha (normal conditions), 225,000 plants/ha (irrigated or high-input), 90,000 plants/ha (sandy, droughty soils).

Fertilizer Application for Pearl Millet

Recommended Doses:
Arid Regions: 40 kg N + 20 kg P2O5/ha Semi-Arid Regions: 60 kg N + 30 kg P2O5/ha
Nitrogen Application:
Light Soils: Apply half the nitrogen dose at seedbed preparation. Apply the remaining half as a side-dressing at 25 days after sowing.
Heavy Soils: Apply all the nitrogen at seedbed preparation.
Fertilizer Placement:
Avoid fertilizer burn: Do not apply fertilizer in the seed furrow or very close to the seed.
Side-dressing: Apply fertilizer as a side-dressing.
Biofertilizers:
Azospirillum and PSB: Use to reduce N and P fertilizer application.
Zinc Application:
Zinc-deficient soils: Apply 10 kg ZnSO4/ha.
Correcting zinc deficiency in standing crop: Spray 0.2% ZnSO4 at tillering to pre-flowering stage.
Water Management:
Prolonged dry spell: Skip top-dressing of N and spray 2% urea.
Excessive rain: Apply additional 20 kg N/ha.

Pearl Millet soil conditions

Soil type:
Millets can grow in a wide range of soil types, including sandy, loamy, and clay soils. However, well-drained soils with good water-holding capacity are preferred. Excessive waterlogging can lead to root rot and other issues.
Soil pH:
Millets prefer slightly acidic to neutral soils with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.5. Acidic soils can be amended with lime to adjust the pH level and optimize nutrient availability.
Soil fertility:
Millets can tolerate low soil fertility to some extent but perform best in soils with moderate to high fertility. Organic matter content is crucial for nutrient retention and moisture management. Adding organic matter through compost or well-rotted manure can improve soil fertility.
Soil moisture:
Millets can adapt to different moisture conditions, from dryland to rainfed cultivation. However, well-distributed moisture during critical growth stages, such as Millet Seeds - Production Technology, Harvesting and Post Harvesting Harvest Management, Processing, flowering and grain filling, is essential for optimal yield. Adequate soil moisture management techniques, including mulching and irrigation, can help maintain optimal soil moisture levels.
